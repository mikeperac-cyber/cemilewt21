IELTS Writing Task 2 Masterclass

Prompt: "In many cities, traffic congestion is becoming increasingly serious. What are the main causes of this problem, and what measures could be taken to solve it?"

💡 Top Tips for "Causes & Solutions" Essays

  • Don't overcomplicate: Stick to two main causes and two practical solutions. It is better to fully develop a few ideas than to simply list many.
  • Maintain clear structure: Dedicate Body Paragraph 1 strictly to Causes and Body Paragraph 2 strictly to Solutions.
  • Connect solutions to causes: Ensure that the solutions you propose logically address the specific causes you mentioned in the first body paragraph.

🧠 Key Points (Brainstorming)

Causes:
  • Overpopulation and rapid urbanization leading to a higher volume of cars.
  • Inadequate public transportation infrastructure, forcing people to rely on private vehicles.
Solutions:
  • Governments should invest heavily in mass transit systems (trains, subways, buses).
  • Implement congestion pricing (toll rings) in city centers to discourage unnecessary driving.

⚙️ Necessary Grammar for Band 7+

To score a 7 or higher, you need a mix of complex sentence structures. Focus on these two structures for this specific essay type:

  • Cause and Effect Structures: Instead of always using "because," use phrases like: "Due to the influx of...", "Consequently,...", "...which inevitably leads to...", "A primary driver of this issue is..."
  • First and Second Conditionals (for Solutions): Use conditional sentences to show the result of a proposed measure.
    Example: "If governments were to subsidize public transport fares, more commuters would be inclined to leave their cars at home."

📝 Band 7+ Essay Template

Introduction:
[Paraphrase the prompt]. This essay will discuss the primary reasons behind this worsening issue, mainly [Briefly state Cause 1] and [Briefly state Cause 2], before proposing viable solutions such as [Briefly state Solution 1] and [Briefly state Solution 2].

Body Paragraph 1 (Causes):
There are several main factors contributing to the rise in vehicular congestion. The most significant cause is [Cause 1]. [Explain Cause 1 - why does this happen?]. For example, [Give a specific example]. Furthermore, another driving factor is [Cause 2]. [Explain Cause 2 and its result].

Body Paragraph 2 (Solutions):
Despite the severity of this issue, there are effective measures that governments and individuals can take to mitigate it. First and foremost, [Solution 1]. [Explain how this solution works]. If [Apply conditional sentence to show the result]. Additionally, [Solution 2]. [Explain the impact of Solution 2].

Conclusion:
In conclusion, while urban traffic gridlock is a pressing problem driven by [Summarize Causes], it can be effectively alleviated by [Summarize Solutions]. If these steps are taken, cities will become far more navigable and sustainable in the future.

Band 7.5 Model Answer

In recent years, vehicular congestion has become an increasingly severe problem in major urban centers around the world. This essay will discuss the primary reasons behind this worsening issue, mainly the over-reliance on private vehicles and inadequate public infrastructure, before proposing viable solutions such as investing in mass transit and implementing congestion pricing.

There are two main factors contributing to the rise in urban gridlock. The most significant cause is the sheer volume of private cars on the road. As urban populations grow and living standards improve, more individuals are purchasing personal vehicles for their daily commutes, leading to a massive influx of traffic during rush hours. Furthermore, another driving factor is subpar public transit systems. In many cities, buses and trains are unreliable, overcrowded, or poorly connected. Consequently, commuters are left with little choice but to drive, which inevitably leads to congested roadways.

Despite the severity of this issue, there are effective measures that governments can take to mitigate it. First and foremost, city municipalities must heavily subsidize and expand mass transit networks. By building more extensive subway lines and ensuring buses run punctually, public transport can become a more attractive option. If governments were to provide a reliable and affordable alternative, a significant portion of motorists would gladly leave their cars at home. Additionally, authorities could implement congestion pricing in city centers. By charging a toll for entering busy districts during peak hours, cities can actively discourage unnecessary driving and reduce the overall volume of traffic.

In conclusion, while urban traffic gridlock is a pressing problem driven by the overuse of private cars and inadequate transport infrastructure, it can be effectively alleviated by expanding public transit and introducing congestion charges. If these steps are implemented proactively, urban areas will become far more navigable and sustainable in the future.
